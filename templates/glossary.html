Algerian Forest Fires Dataset

Data Set Information:

The dataset includes 244 instances that regroup data of two regions of Algeria, namely the Bejaia region located in the northeast of Algeria and the Sidi Bel-abbes region located in the northwest of Algeria.

  • 122 instances for each region.
  • The period from June 2012 to September 2012.
  • The dataset includes 11 attributes and 1 output attribute (class).
  • The 244 instances have been classified into fire (138 classes) and not fire (106 classes) classes.

Fire Weather Indices

Understanding fire risk factors and their measurements

This glossary provides definitions and explanations of important terms used in fire weather forecasting and risk assessment. Understanding these indices can help in interpreting fire danger ratings and preparing for potential fire hazards.

Environmental Factors

+

Temperature (Â°C) Higher temperatures accelerate fuel drying and increase fire risk potential.

Humidity (%) Lower relative humidity indicates drier conditions, making vegetation more combustible.

Wind Speed (km/h) Strong winds increase oxygen supply and rapidly spread fires across landscapes.

Rain (mm) Precipitation increases fuel moisture content, reducing flammability and fire spread.

Fire Weather Indices

+

FFMC Fine Fuel Moisture Code - Indicates the dryness of fine surface fuels. Higher values (>90) indicate highly flammable conditions.

DMC Duff Moisture Code - Measures moisture in moderate-depth organic layers and medium-sized woody debris.

DC Drought Code - Long-term dryness indicator reflecting deep soil moisture deficits and large log moisture.

ISI Initial Spread Index - Represents the expected rate of fire spread, combining wind and FFMC effects.

BUI Buildup Index - Indicates total fuel available for combustion, combining DMC and DC values.

FWI Fire Weather Index - Overall fire danger rating integrating all indices into a single risk metric.
